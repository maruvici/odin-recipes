How To Make: Pork Sisig

Description

Pork Sisig is a sizzling Filipino dish made from chopped pork parts, usually pork belly or face, seasoned with calamansi, onions, chili, and spices. Itâ€™s known for its savory, tangy, and slightly spicy flavor, often served on a sizzling plate with a raw egg on top. Crispy, flavorful, and perfect with rice or as pulutan (beer food), sisig is a true Filipino favorite.

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly
  • Minced Onions
  • Chopped Red and Green Chili Peppers
  • Calamansi or Lemon Juice
  • Soy Sauce
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Cooking Oil or Butter
  • Mayonnaise (Optional)
  • Egg (Optional)

Steps

  1. Boil the pork belly or face until tender, then grill or broil until the skin is crisp and slightly charred.
  2. Chop the cooked pork into small pieces and place in a bowl.
  3. Add minced onions, chopped chilies, soy sauce, calamansi juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
  4. Heat oil or butter in a sizzling plate or pan, then add the pork mixture and cook until hot and slightly crispy.
  5. Optional: Stir in a spoonful of mayonnaise for added creaminess.
  6. Top with a raw egg just before serving, allowing it to cook from the heat of the plate.
  7. Serve sizzling and enjoy with rice or as a side dish with drinks.
