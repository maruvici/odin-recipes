How To Make: Lumpiang Shanghai

Description

Lumpiang Shanghai is a Filipino-style spring roll filled with a savory mixture of ground pork, vegetables, and seasonings, wrapped in thin crepes and deep-fried until golden an crisp. Often served with a sweet and sour dipping sauce, itâ€™s a favorite party appetizer or everyday meal companion. Its crunchy texture and flavorful filling make it one of the most beloved Filipino dishes.

Ingredients

  • Ground pork
  • Chopped Carrots
  • Minced Onions
  • Minced Garlic
  • Chopped Green Onions
  • Egg
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Spring Roll (Lumpia) Wrappers
  • Cooking Oil

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine ground pork, chopped carrots, onions, garlic, green onions, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix well until evenly blended.
  2. Place a spoonful of the mixture onto a lumpia wrapper and shape it into a thin log near one corner.
  3. Fold the sides inward and roll tightly, sealing the edge with a dab of water or beaten egg.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and deep-fry the rolls until golden brown and crisp.
  5. Remove from oil, drain on paper towels, and let cool slightly before serving.
  6. Serve with sweet and sour sauce or banana ketchup for dipping.
