How To Make: Halo-Halo

Description

Halo-halo is a popular Filipino dessert made with a colorful mix of sweet ingredients served over crushed ice and milk. Its name means “mix-mix,” reflecting how it’s enjoyed by combining all the flavors and textures together. Refreshing, vibrant, and indulgent, halo-halo perfectly captures the spirit of Filipino creativity and love for sweet treats.

Ingredients

  • Crushed Ice
  • Evaporated Milk
  • Leche Flan (Caramel Custard)
  • Ube Halaya or Ice Cream
  • Sweetened Saba Bananas
  • Sweetened Langka (Jackfruit)
  • Sweetened Garbanzos (Chickpeas)
  • Macapuno (Sweetened Coconut)
  • Nata de Coco (Coconut Gel)
  • Kaong (Sugar Palm Fruit)
  • Sweetened White Beans
  • Pinipig
  • Gulaman Cubes

Steps

  1. Prepare a tall glass or bowl for serving.
  2. Add small portions of sweetened fruits and jellies (e.g. langka, saba bananas, kaong, etc.) at the bottom.
  3. Add a layer of shaved or crushed ice over the ingredients.
  4. Pour evaporated milk evenly over the ice.
  5. Top with leche flan, ube halaya, or a scoop of ube ice cream.
  6. Sprinkle pinipig on top for extra crunch.
  7. Serve immediately and mix before eating!
